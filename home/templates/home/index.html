Google Ads (formerly Google AdWords and Google AdWords Express) is an online advertising solution that businesses use to promote their products and services on Google Search, YouTube, and other sites across the web. Google Ads also allows advertisers to choose specific goals for their ads, like driving phone calls or website visits. With a Google Ads account, advertisers can customize their budgets and targeting, and start or stop their ads at any time.

Once your ad is approved, it can appear whenever users in your target area search for a product or service like yours. You only pay when users engage with your ad, like clicking your ad or calling your business.

To get started with Google Ads, Our guided setup process will walk you through creating your first ad in just a few steps. If you need help signing up, our Google Ads experts are available to set up your new account and even assist in creating your first campaign at no additional cost.

There are three basic types of Google Ads: Search Network campaigns - usually text form, these ads can show on Google Search results pages when someone searches for a product or service that’s similar to yours Display Network campaigns - usually image form, these ads appear on websites or apps that your customers visit Video campaigns - usually 6 or 15 second videos, these ads show right before or during YouTube content

CPC (Cost Per Click) or PPC (Pay Per Click) means you only pay for an ad if someone clicks on it. Other advertising models include: Cost Per Impression, where you pay based on how many times your ad was shown (not clicked) Cost Per Engagement, where you pay when a user completes a predefined engagement (like watching your video ad)
